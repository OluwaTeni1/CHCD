About Us

CHCD-UK is a non-profit organization dedicated to bridging the gap between the Gospel and social issues, fostering wholistic transformation in communities.

We work with local partners to create sustainable development initiatives that address both spiritual and physical needs, empowering communities to thrive.

Our approach combines faith-based values with evidence-based practices to create lasting change in the most vulnerable communities.

Our Core Objectives

  • Disciple-making community for every people and place

  • Kingdom impact in every sphere of society Work History

Our Journey

In the year 2014 and 2015 our Team East Africa (TEA-CHCD) was in five Counties in Kenya and a county in Uganda for research and engagement. Nairobi, Kakamega, Busia, Narok, Mombasa of Kenya and Busia Uganda counties were the province of engagement respectively. About thirty local leaders were engaged in Mumias of Kakamega County with ideation on how to facilitate development and economic growth through various opportunities which seemingly was categorized as development challenge and gaps. In Siginga of Busia-Kenya County about fifteen widows and widower were engaged and empowered through cropping and animal husbandry (Pig rearing), fifteen community leaders were engaged in Port-Victoria (a district under Busia County), while twenty were engaged in Busia city of Busia County of Western Kenya to take responsibility for the sustainable development of their communities which became an in-road to building self-propagating faith communities.

Our community development work started in Nigeria in the year 2015. Through research. We explore the causes of socio-economic problems which are poverty, illiteracy, high mortality rate and how they affect the family and community life, as well as the environmental sustenance in spite of the several assets, human and economic capital untapped among the Jarawa people of Jos East Local Government, Plateau State, Nigeria. Jarawa tribe also known as Afizere is the first tribe to be engaged with Community Development and Discipleship initiative, a development model that abridged the community driven development with community-based development models, addressing the gaps in the two development models.

165 villages in Jos East Local Government Area were engaged, envisioned, entrusted and empowered with exposure to community development ideation, capacity building and trainings to facilitate wholistic transformation process through economic support of three hundred households. This is to help envision a desired-future for communities and to maximize local assets available within their reach to attain the desired height.

While our engagement is growing in East and West Africa, we remained focus to serve Northern region of Africa countries while expanding our engagement across the continents of the world especially among the least evangelized with unreached people groups (tribes with less than 2% followers of Jesus) across Africa.
